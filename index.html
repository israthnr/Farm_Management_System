Crop Information

Corn (Maize)

Corn is a warm-season crop that requires a long growing season and plenty of sunlight. It is rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and essential nutrients.

Wheat

Wheat is a cool-season crop that grows best in temperate regions. It is a good source of complex carbohydrates, protein, and dietary fiber.

Soybeans

Soybeans are a versatile crop that can be used for food, animal feed, and industrial purposes. They are an excellent source of plant-based protein and contain healthy fats.

Rice

Rice is a staple food for billions of people worldwide and is a primary crop in many Asian countries. It is a good source of energy and provides essential nutrients.

Cotton

Cotton is a major cash crop used in the textile industry for making fabrics, clothing, and other products. It is known for its softness, breathability, and durability.

Potatoes

Potatoes are an important staple crop worldwide and are known for their high carbohydrate content. They are versatile and can be cooked in various ways, such as boiling, frying, or baking.

Tomatoes

Tomatoes are a popular fruit that is often considered a vegetable in culinary applications. They are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.

Soil Information

Types Of Soil In Agriculture To Grow Crops Efficiently


When it comes to crop production, considering the types of soil is essential. Six main soil types are typically distinguished in agriculture. Each of them has specific physical and chemical properties.some types of soil are more productive and accommodating, utilizing amendments suitable for each type can still benefit growers.

The state of a field constantly changes due to various causes and must be regularly examined. Satellite technologies can be advantageous for pinpointing areas requiring immediate rehabilitation and tracking changes over time.



Amendments for different soil types

Types of Soil Amendments
Sand Plenty of organic mulches, peat moss, compost, zeolite, or bentonite clay.
Clay Compost and other organic matter.
Slit Drainage aid particles, such as sand or compost.
Loam Any organic amendments at least once a year.
Chalk Mulching around plant crowns and plenty of organic matter, such as humus, manure, composted pine needles, or leaf mold.
Peat Sand, compost, mulch, and other drain-improving materials.
